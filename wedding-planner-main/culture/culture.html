Kannadigas

Kannadigas are people from the state of Karnataka in South India, and they have a rich and diverse culture.The "Kashi Yatra" ceremony is an important custom in which the groom pretends to leave for the city of Kashi (Varanasi) in search of spiritual knowledge, but the bride's father convinces him to return and marry his daughter.The bride and groom exchange garlands and take seven vows to signify their commitment to each other.

Telugu

Telugu weddings are traditional weddings that take place in the Telugu-speaking regions of India. They are typically multi-day events with several ceremonies and rituals, including the "Jeelakarra Bellam," "Kanyadaanam," and "Saptapadi" ceremonies. The "Mangalsutra Dharana" ceremony involves the groom tying a sacred thread around the bride's neck, symbolizing their union.The wedding is an occasion for family and friends to come together and celebrate with music, dance, and delicious vegetarian food.

Tamilians

Tamil weddings are traditional weddings that take place in the Tamil-speaking regions of India, including the state of Tamil Nadu.Tamil weddings are typically simple and elegant affairs that take place over one or two days.The bride and groom exchange garlands, and the groom ties a sacred thread around the bride's neck in a ceremony called "Mangalsutra Dharanam." The "Kanyadaanam" ceremony is where the father of the bride gives her hand in marriage to the groom. The wedding concludes with the "Saptapadi" ritual, where the bride and groom take seven vows and seven steps around the sacred fire, signifying their commitment to each other.

Mallu

Take place in the state of Kerala, located in the southern part of India.Malayalam weddings are usually simple and elegant affairs that take place over one or two days. The wedding ceremony is typically held in a temple or a wedding hall, and the bride and groom exchange garlands in a ceremony called "Maala Dharanam." The "Kanyadaanam" ceremony is where the father of the bride gives her hand in marriage to the groom. The "Mangalsutra Dharanam" ceremony symbolizing their union. The "Sadhya" feast, a traditional vegetarian meal served on a banana leaf, is an important part of the wedding celebration.

Punjabi

Punjabi weddings are traditional weddings that take place in the northern region of India, in the state of Punjab and weddings are grand affairs that last several days. The pre-wedding celebrations include the "Roka" and "Sangeet" ceremonies. The wedding ceremony takes place under a decorated "mandap." The "Milni" ceremony involves the exchange of garlands and gifts. The "Anand Karaj" ceremony is a Sikh wedding ritual. The wedding concludes with the "Vidaai" ceremony. The reception is a celebration with music, dance, and delicious Punjabi food.
